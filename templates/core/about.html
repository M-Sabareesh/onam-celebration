The Legend of King Mahabali (Maveli)

Onam is the most important and popular festival in Kerala, celebrating the homecoming of the legendary King Mahabali (Maveli), whose reign was considered the golden age of Kerala.

The Golden Age

According to Hindu mythology, King Mahabali was a just and benevolent ruler under whose reign Kerala experienced unprecedented prosperity, peace, and happiness. During his rule:

  • There was no crime, poverty, or inequality
  • People lived in harmony and prosperity
  • Truth and justice prevailed throughout the land
  • Everyone was equal regardless of caste or creed

When is Onam Celebrated?

Onam falls during the Malayalam month of Chingam (August-September) and is celebrated for 10 days. The most important day is Thiruvonam, which marks the homecoming of King Mahabali to visit his beloved people.

Harvest Festival

Onam is also a harvest festival that celebrates the rice harvest and the bounty of nature in "God's Own Country" - Kerala. It brings together people of all communities to celebrate the rich cultural heritage and traditions of Kerala.

Onam Traditions & Celebrations

Discover the beautiful traditions that make Onam special

Pookalam (Flower Carpet)

Beautiful flower carpets created in front of homes to welcome King Mahabali. These intricate designs use different colored flowers and are a symbol of Onam's beauty and the artistic heritage of Kerala.

Onam Sadhya (Feast)

The traditional Onam feast served on banana leaves, featuring rice and multiple vegetarian dishes, pickles, and payasam (sweet pudding). It represents the abundance during King Mahabali's reign.

Pulikali (Tiger Dance)

The colorful tiger dance where performers paint themselves as tigers and leopards, dancing to the rhythm of traditional percussion instruments like chenda and thakil.

Kathakali & Cultural Arts

Classical dance drama of Kerala featuring elaborate costumes, makeup, and storytelling through dance and expressions, celebrating the rich cultural heritage.
